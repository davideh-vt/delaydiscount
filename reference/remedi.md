# REMEDI data

Delay discounting survey data from 434 adult subjects who reported
having obesity and type 2 diabetes.

## Usage

``` r
remedi
```

## Format

### `remedi`

A data frame with 3,038 rows and 5 columns:

- study:

  Study name (always REMEDI)

- subj:

  Subject ID Number

- group:

  Condition group

- delay:

  The wait in days to receive the reward,

- indiff:

  The requisite proportion of reward needed for the subject to be
  indifferent to waiting for the full reward or receiving a reduced
  reward immediately

## Source

Brown, et al. (2023). "Episodic future thinking in type 2 diabetes:
Further development and validation of the Health Information Thinking
control for clinical trials". *PLoS ONE 18*(8): e0289478
<https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0289478>
